Example Program:

* Make a program to read the measures of the sides of two triangles X and Y (assume valid measures). Then show the value of the areas of the two triangles and tell which of the two triangles has the greater area.

The formula for calculating the area of a triangle from the measures of its sides a, b, and c is as follows (Heron's formula):

area = (sqrt) p (p-a) x (p-b) x (p-c)

where

p = a + b + c / 2
